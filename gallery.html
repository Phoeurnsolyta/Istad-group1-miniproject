Paintings

Apsara dancers

Through July 20

This painting captures the elegance and spirituality of the celestial Apsaras in Khmer mythology. With graceful movements and intricate costuming, it reflects the deep cultural roots of Cambodian classical dance and textile art, especially from Angkor Wat.

Hanuman Arts of Reamker

June 28-Ongoing

Hanuman, the white monkey general, is a central hero in the Reamker, Cambodia’s version of the Ramayana. Often shown in dynamic poses—leaping, fighting, or bowing in devotion—Hanuman represents bravery, loyalty, and divine wisdom. Khmer artists capture his spirit through detailed carvings, murals, and dance, making him one of the most beloved figures in classical Khmer art and mythology.

Sculptures

Sleeping Vishnu: Musée Guimet’s Paris, France

July 17, 2025–May 31, 2026

This sculpture depicts the Hindu god Vishnu in a reclining pose on the cosmic serpent Ananta. A symbol of cosmic balance and the eternal cycle of creation, this image is a spiritual cornerstone in Khmer temple art, reflecting deep Hindu-Buddhist fusion in Khmer belief.

King Jayavarman VII

Through February 15, 2026

This sandstone statue represents King Jayavarman VII, the most powerful and devout Buddhist ruler of the Khmer Empire. Seated in deep meditation, the sculpture reflects his dedication to Mahayana Buddhism and his vision of a compassionate, divine kingship.

Ancient Khmer bronze vessel Musée Guimet's Paris, France

This intricately cast bronze vessel from the Angkorian period (9th–13th century) was used in religious rituals, possibly for holding sacred water or offerings. Its fine detailing and balanced form reflect the Khmer people's deep spiritual beliefs and advanced metalworking techniques.

Bas-Relief Art

Khmer Temple Bas-Relief

May 27-Ongoing

A form of sculpture where images are carved slightly from a flat surface, bas-relief brings stories to life through depth and detail. Common in Khmer temples, it was used to depict gods, legends, and daily life—turning stone walls into sacred narratives.
